GESTATIONAL DIABETES

Introduction

    Gestational diabetes is a type of diabetes that develops during pregnancy and usually disappears after giving birth. It occurs when the body cannot produce enough insulin to meet the increased needs during pregnancy. This leads to higher blood sugar levels, which can pose risks to both the mother and baby if not managed properly.

Insights

  • The likelihood of being diagnosed with GDM is higher among women who are categorized as overweight, compared to those in other BMI categories..
  • Age is a significant risk factor for developing GDM during pregnancy. GDM is more prominent in middle age to older aged women. Women over 30 years of age with GDM 73%.
  • Patients with GDM in their previous pregnancy are 57% more prone to have GDM in the current pregnancy and more likely to develop Type 2 diabetes in future.
  • previous GDM, BMI, Vit D deficiency, smoking status are contributing to GDM
